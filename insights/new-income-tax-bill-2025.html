Income Tax Updates

Key Highlights of the New Income Tax Bill, 2025

Published on August 18, 2025 by CA Sagar H R • 6 min read

The Indian Parliament has passed the new Income Tax Bill, 2025, a landmark piece of legislation set to replace the long-standing Income Tax Act of 1961. This new bill aims to simplify and modernize India's direct tax system, making it more taxpayer-friendly and aligned with the country's economic goals. The new law will come into effect on April 1, 2026.

Key Takeaways

  • The new bill introduces a single "tax year" concept, replacing the separate "financial year" and "assessment year".
  • Tax-free income limit raised to ₹12 lakhs for individuals under the new tax regime via rebate.
  • Standard deduction for salaried individuals increased to ₹75,000.
  • Taxpayers can now claim TDS refunds even on belatedly filed returns.

Simplification and Clarity

One of the primary objectives of the new bill is to reduce the complexity of the existing tax law. The text has been shortened by nearly half, and the number of sections and chapters has been significantly reduced. This is expected to streamline the tax filing process and reduce compliance burdens for individuals and businesses.

Major Changes for Taxpayers

The bill introduces several changes that will directly impact taxpayers:

  • Increased Tax Rebate: The income tax rebate under Section 87A for those opting for the new tax regime has been increased. Now, individuals with an income of up to ₹12 lakhs will have zero tax liability.
  • Higher Standard Deduction: The standard deduction for salaried individuals under the new tax regime has been increased from ₹50,000 to ₹75,000.
  • Enhanced Family Pension Deduction: The deduction for family pension has also been raised from ₹15,000 to ₹25,000.
  • Relief on Late Filing: In a significant move, taxpayers will now be able to claim TDS refunds even if they file their income tax returns after the statutory deadline.

Provisions for Businesses & Non-profits

The new bill also includes important provisions for businesses and non-profit organizations:

  • MSME Alignment: The bill aligns the definition of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) with the MSME Act, ensuring consistency across different laws.
  • Loss Carry Forward: The existing provisions for carrying forward and setting off losses have been retained, providing stability for businesses.
  • Relief for Non-profits: The bill provides tax relief on anonymous donations to religious and charitable trusts, ensuring that such contributions do not affect their eligibility for tax exemptions.

This new legislation marks a significant step towards a more efficient and equitable tax system in India. As we approach the implementation date, it is crucial for all taxpayers to understand these changes and their implications.
