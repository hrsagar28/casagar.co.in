Privacy Policy

Effective Date / Last Updated: 26 August 2025

1. Introduction and scope

Sagar H R & Co., Chartered Accountants (“the Firm”, “we”, “us”, “our”), acting as a Data Fiduciary , is committed to protecting Personal Data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, disclose and protect Personal Data via our website www.casagar.co.in (the “Site”) and related services, in accordance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (“DPDP Act”) and other applicable law. By using the Site you accept the terms of this Policy.

2. Definitions

  • Personal Data: information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
  • Data Principal: the person to whom Personal Data relates.
  • Data Fiduciary: the entity that determines the purpose and means of Processing (the Firm).
  • Data Processor: a third party that Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Data Fiduciary.
  • Processing: any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means (including collection, recording, organisation, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, erasure or destruction).

(Defined terms are capitalised throughout.)

3. Categories of Personal Data collected

We limit collection to what is necessary. Categories include:

  • Data you provide: name; e-mail address; telephone number; company/organisation; communications and any other data you submit via contact forms, e-mail or recruitment applications.
  • Technical/usage data: IP address; browser and device information; operating system; pages visited and timestamps; server logs.
  • Sensitive data: We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data (e.g., financial account credentials, health data) via the Site except where voluntarily provided pursuant to a formal engagement and only to the extent necessary for that engagement.

4. Lawful basis and purposes for Processing

We Process Personal Data on lawful bases under the DPDP Act, including:

  • Consent: where you have provided free, specific, informed, unconditional and unambiguous consent (s 6(1), DPDP Act).
  • Legitimate use — performance of contract: processing necessary for performance of any contract with the Data Principal (s 7(1)(a), DPDP Act) or to take pre-contractual steps at the Data Principal’s request.
  • Compliance with law: processing required to comply with legal or regulatory obligations.
  • Permitted legitimate uses: fraud prevention, security, internal administration and comparable operational purposes permitted by law.

The Firm does not presently qualify as a “Significant Data Fiduciary.” If our status changes, we will comply with any additional statutory obligations.

5. Purpose limitation and data minimisation

Personal Data is processed only for the specified, explicit and legitimate purposes and only to the extent necessary. We will not process Personal Data for incompatible purposes without additional lawful basis and notice.

6. Retention of Personal Data

We retain Personal Data no longer than necessary and, in any event, for the later of: (a) completion of the purpose for which it was collected; (b) expiry of applicable statutory limitation periods; or (c) the period required for the exercise or defence of legal, tax or regulatory claims.

Indicative retention schedule (mobile-friendly):

  • Contact form / general enquiries: up to 2 years from last contact, or until consent is withdrawn, whichever is earlier .
  • Marketing / newsletter lists: until you withdraw consent.
  • Prospective client records (no engagement): up to 5 years for risk management.
  • Client engagement files and audit working papers: not less than 7 years from completion of the engagement.
  • Books of account and statutory records (Companies Act / Income-tax Act): not less than 8 years from the end of the relevant financial year , or longer where statute requires.

Retention may be extended where necessary to preserve evidence or to comply with litigation, tax investigations or regulatory obligations.

7. Cookies and similar technologies

Status: We do not presently use third-party analytics or marketing cookies on the Site. The Site may use strictly necessary, first-party cookies to enable technical functionality and to remember your consent choice. The cookie consent banner’s sole function is to store your consent choice; it does not track you.

Examples of first-party cookies used to record consent: cookie_terms_accepted, cookie_warning_dismissed, _tccl_visitor. If we introduce non-essential tracking, we will implement a granular consent mechanism and update this Policy.

You can disable cookies via your browser; essential cookies may be required for Site functionality.

8. Data Processors, transfers and safeguards

We engage Data Processors (hosting providers, email providers, cloud vendors, professional advisers) under written Data Processing Agreements mandating confidentiality, appropriate technical and organisational measures, and processing only on our documented instructions. We will transfer Personal Data outside India only to jurisdictions not notified as restricted under s 16 DPDP Act, or pursuant to an exemption or lawful mechanism prescribed by the Central Government. Where required by foreign law (e.g., GDPR) we will apply additional safeguards as applicable.

9. Disclosure and lawful disclosure

We do not sell Personal Data. We may disclose Personal Data to: authorised Data Processors; professional advisers and auditors; courts, regulators or law enforcement where required by law; and other parties with your explicit consent.

10. Security and Personal Data breach notification

We maintain reasonable technical, administrative and physical safeguards to protect Personal Data. In the event of a Personal Data breach affecting data within our control, we will activate our incident response procedure and notify the competent authority (Data Protection Board) and affected Data Principals without undue delay and in accordance with any time-frames prescribed by the Central Government or the Data Protection Board. We will take reasonable steps to mitigate harm and prevent recurrence.

11. Rights of Data Principals; how to exercise them

Subject to applicable law, Data Principals have the right to: confirmation of processing and a summary of processing; access; correction; erasure where lawful; grievance redressal; and nomination of a representative. You may nominate, in such manner as may be prescribed, any other individual to exercise your rights in the event of your death or incapacity (s 12(5), DPDP Act). (See Section 14 for contact details.)

Certain additional rights (e.g., portability) may apply under foreign laws; those rights will be honoured where required.

To exercise your rights, contact the Grievance Officer (Section 14). We will acknowledge valid requests promptly and endeavour to respond within 30 days , or sooner if law prescribes.

12. Children’s data

The Site is not directed to persons under 18. We do not knowingly process Personal Data of children under 18 without verifiable parental consent and do not undertake targeted advertising directed at children.
